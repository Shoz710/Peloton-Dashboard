🌀 The Peloton Death Spiral — How It Works

1Churn RisesFewer new hardware sales = fewer new subscribers. Existing members cancel.
2Sub Revenue FallsSubscription revenue ($44/mo per sub) declines with the base.
3Fixed Costs PersistContent, studios, instructors, and $1.5B debt are largely fixed.
4Less InvestmentLower revenue → less R&D, fewer new features, weaker content.
5More ChurnWeaker product → more cancellations → back to Step 1.

📉 Subscriber Erosion

−176K subs YoY

Paid CF Subscriptions fell from 2.976M (FY24) to 2.800M (FY25), a 6% decline. Q1 FY26 guidance projects a further decline to 2.72–2.73M. Each lost subscriber costs ~$528/yr in subscription revenue at $44/mo.

🔄 Churn Acceleration

1.6% avg monthly churn

Annual churn rose from 1.3% (FY23) → 1.4% (FY24) → 1.6% (FY25). At 1.6% monthly, Peloton loses ~19% of its subscriber base annually before new additions. Q4 FY25 churn spiked to 1.8% seasonally.

🏋️ Hardware Dependency

−17.6% hardware revenue

Connected Fitness Products revenue fell from $991.7M (FY24) to $817.1M (FY25). New hardware sales are the primary driver of new subscriber additions. Without hardware growth, subscriber replenishment stalls.

💰 Debt Burden

$1.50B total debt

$990M Term Loan (10.9% effective rate) + $199M 2026 Convertible Notes (maturing Feb 2026) + $350M 2029 Notes. Annual interest expense: $134.5M. Net debt of $459M constrains strategic flexibility.

📊 Revenue Covenant Risk

$1.2B sub rev covenant

The revolving credit facility requires subscription revenue ≥$1.2B (trailing 4 quarters) if drawn. FY25 subscription revenue was $1.67B — providing headroom, but continued subscriber decline narrows the buffer.

✅ Positive Signals

$403.6M Adj. EBITDA

FY25 Adj. EBITDA surged from $3.5M to $403.6M. Free Cash Flow turned positive at $323.7M. Subscription gross margin improved to 69.1%. Net debt reduced 43% YoY. Q4 FY25 was the first quarter of positive GAAP operating income ($29.6M).

⚠️ 3rd Restructuring Plan

3 plans in 3 years

The 2025 Restructuring Plan (announced Aug 2025) targets $100M+ in run-rate savings by end of FY26 through headcount reductions and retail exits. This is the third consecutive restructuring, signaling persistent structural cost issues.

🎯 FY26 Guidance

Revenue guided ↓ 2%

FY26 Total Revenue guided at $2.4–$2.5B (−2% at midpoint). Paid CF Subs expected to continue declining YoY. Q1 FY26 revenue guided at $525–545M (−9% YoY). First-ever positive full-year operating income targeted.
